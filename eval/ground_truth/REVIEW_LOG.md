## Summary of Conventions That Emerged During Review

The following new conventions emerged during review and have been added to `LABELING_CONVENTIONS.md`:

1. **Functional Expertise — strict interpretation** (cv_15)
   - Only count operationally performed activities; do not derive from related terms, MBA specializations, or industry knowledge alone

2. **Career Level for Career-Switchers** (cv_15)
   - View B: career_level is based on experience in the target profession, not on total professional experience
   - total_years_experience remains the total count; only career_level follows the target-profession logic

3. **Total Years — mathematical rounding** (cv_17)
   - 8.5 years → 9 years (round half up)

4. **Part-time as calendar time** (cv_16)
   - Part-time roles count as calendar time, not as FTE-equivalent

5. **Skill levels for tools — out of scope for v1** (general)
   - "Python (advanced)" → only "Python" extracted
   - Self-assessments are inconsistent across applicants; v2-roadmap item

6. **Academic grades — out of scope for v1** (general)
   - "summa cum laude", "First Class Honours", GPA values are not extracted
   - Regional normalization (DE 1.0-5.0, UK First/Upper Second, US 4.0) is v2 work
   - Important to note: grades are a primary filter in consulting recruiting

7. **CFA Candidate convention** (cv_04, cv_05, cv_07)
   - "CFA Level X Candidate" is NOT included (not completed)
   - "CFA Level X (passed)" is included

8. **German B2 and below as unmapped** (cv_18)
   - Persona-based decision: a German consultancy rarely receives serious applications with German below C1
   - Languages outside taxonomy (Hindi, Dutch, etc.) → unmapped

## Generalized Learning From This Review

**AI-pre-labeling of ground truth tends systematically toward liberal interpretation:**
- Functional Expertise is derived from mentioned terms (cv_15: "regulated takeovers" → incorrectly mapped to "Risk Management")
- Operational activities are derived from MBA specializations (cv_15: MBA "Strategy" → incorrectly mapped to Functional Expertise "Strategy")

**Mitigation:** Human review must actively ask "Has the candidate DEMONSTRABLY performed this operationally?", not just "Is something related mentioned somewhere in the text?".

**Generalizes to pipeline:** If the pipeline shows the same behavior (LLM extracts "Strategy" from MBA mention), we need either:
- Stricter prompts ("Extract only operationally performed expertise, not derived from education or context")
- Or a self-critique loop in v2 (LLM checks its own output against the strict definition)

**AI-pre-labeling fails at temporal mapping of projects to roles:**
   - When a CV lists multiple projects under one role section, the labeler tends to either pick only the first project, or fail to allocate projects across role timelines.
   - Generalizes to pipeline: project-to-role attribution requires temporal reasoning, not just text extraction. May need a dedicated post-processing step in v2.
